A Simple Guide to Staying Safe

Phishing is a deceptive technique used to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information. It is "Social Engineering"—a hack of human behavior rather than software.

Primary Goals: Credential theft, financial fraud, and malware distribution.

Scammers exploit emotions to bypass critical thinking:

  • Urgency: Forced action via fake deadlines.
  • Fear: Threats of account suspension or legal action.
  • Greed: Promises of unearned rewards or prizes.

Understanding how links are built is key to spotting lies.

https://microsoft.com.security-check.xyz/login

In this example, xyz is the real domain. The "microsoft.com" part is a deceptive subdomain.

Homoglyphs are characters that look identical but are mathematically different.

Real: apple.com
Fake: аpple.com

The "a" in the fake link is Cyrillic. Browsers see this as: xn--pple-43d.com.

  • Smishing: Phishing via SMS.
  • Vishing: Phishing via voice/phone calls.
  • Quishing: Malicious QR codes.

BEC (Business Email Compromise): Impersonating executives to authorize fraudulent wire transfers.

  1. MFA: Always use app-based authenticators.
  2. Manual Navigation: Never click links for sensitive actions; type the URL directly.
  3. Password Managers: Use unique, complex credentials for every service.
